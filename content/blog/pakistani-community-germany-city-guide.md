Moving to a new country is hard enough. Moving to a country where you do not speak the language, cannot find your favourite daal chawal, and have no idea where the nearest mosque is? That can feel isolating in ways you did not expect.

The good news: the **Pakistani community in Germany** is larger, more established, and more welcoming than most newcomers realize. With approximately **140,000 Pakistanis living in Germany**, you are joining a diaspora that has been building roots here since the 1960s. Germany is home to one of the largest Pakistani communities in Europe, second only to the United Kingdom.

This guide is your city-by-city map to finding Pakistani restaurants, halal grocery stores, mosques with Urdu or English khutbahs, community organizations, and cultural events. Whether you just landed last week or have been here for years, this will help you find your people.

If you are still in the early stages of settling in, check out our [First 30 Days in Germany Checklist](/blog/first-30-days-germany-checklist-pakistanis/) for a step-by-step plan.

## The Pakistani Diaspora in Germany: A Quick Overview

Pakistani migration to Germany began in the 1960s and 1970s when workers came as part of guest worker programs. Over the decades, the community has grown through family reunification, student migration, and skilled professional immigration, particularly through the Blue Card program.

**Key facts about Pakistanis in Germany:**

- **Estimated population:** Around 140,000 people of Pakistani origin
- **Largest concentration:** Almost a third of all Pakistanis in Germany live in **Hesse** (the state containing Frankfurt)
- **Major cities:** Frankfurt am Main (~1,500), Hamburg (~1,900), Berlin (~1,400), with significant populations in Munich, Cologne, Stuttgart, and Düsseldorf
- **Student population:** Pakistani student enrollment in German universities reached approximately 8,200 by 2023, and continues to grow
- **Community profile:** Mix of long-established families (second and third generation), recent skilled workers, students, and entrepreneurs

The community is well-organized, with cultural associations, mosques, and social groups in every major city. Let us walk through them one by one.

## How to Find Pakistani Community Resources in Any German City

Before we dive into specific cities, here are universal strategies that work everywhere in Germany:

### Google Maps Is Your Best Friend

Search for "Pakistani restaurant" or "halal grocery" or "mosque" on Google Maps in your city. Filter by reviews and ratings. Pakistani and South Asian businesses often appear under broader categories like "Indian restaurant" — read the reviews to find out if they serve Pakistani food specifically.

### Facebook Groups

Almost every German city with a Pakistani population has at least one Facebook group. Search for "[City name] Pakistanis" or "Pakistanis in [City]". These groups are goldmines for recommendations, event announcements, and finding flatmates. Some key groups:

- **Pakistanis Community in Deutschland** — the largest nationwide group
- **Pakistani Community Germany (PCG)** — active community with city-specific subgroups
- **Pakistanis in Berlin** — city-specific group with active discussions

### WhatsApp Communities

Most community organizations and mosque committees run WhatsApp groups. Ask at your local mosque or Pakistani restaurant, and you will be added to the relevant groups within days. University Pakistani student societies also maintain active WhatsApp groups for newcomers.

### Apps for Finding Halal Food and Mosques

- **HalalTrip** and **Zabihah** — find halal restaurants and shops
- **Muslim Pro** and **Athan** — prayer times calibrated to your German city
- **Google Maps** — search "halal" to find nearby options

## City-by-City Guide

### Berlin

Berlin has a thriving Pakistani food scene and a growing community, particularly in neighbourhoods like Neukölln, Wedding, Kreuzberg, and Gesundbrunnen.

**Pakistani and South Asian Restaurants**

Berlin offers some of the best Pakistani food in Germany. Notable spots include:

- **Shalimar** (Gesundbrunnen) — One of Berlin's most established Pakistani restaurants, known for authentic biryani, karahi, and naan fresh from the tandoor. Consistently highly rated.
- **Punjabi Zaiqa** (Wedding, near U Osloer Strasse) — Serves biryanis, naan, curries, and grilled meats. Popular with the local Pakistani community for its home-style cooking.
- **Mann-o-Salwa** (Neukölln) — Home-style Pakistani cuisine. The name itself ("manna from heaven") tells you they take their food seriously.
- **Ajwa Restaurant** — Highly rated Pakistani dining with generous portions.
- **Gujrat Spice Restaurant** — Known for authentic Punjabi flavours, rated 9.2 on TheFork.
- **Hol! Cow - Pakistan Street Food** (Neukölln) — A more casual, street-food oriented option.

Berlin's Pakistani restaurant scene is concentrated in Neukölln and Wedding. Walk down Sonnenallee or Karl-Marx-Strasse and you will find multiple options within a few blocks.

**Halal Grocery Stores**

Berlin has Germany's first dedicated Pakistani superstore, and numerous halal grocery shops throughout the city. Neukölln and Kreuzberg are particularly well-served. You will find stores selling Pakistani spices, atta (flour), lentils, frozen parathas, and halal meat. Sonnenallee (known locally as "Arab Street") has dozens of halal butchers and Middle Eastern grocery stores where you can find most Pakistani staples.

For online delivery, **Tukwila** (tukwila.de) and **Sta Dukaan** (sta-dukaan.de) deliver Pakistani and South Asian groceries across Germany, including to Berlin.

**Mosques**

Berlin has over 80 mosques. While most conduct khutbahs in Turkish or Arabic, several cater to the South Asian community with Urdu or English sermons. Search for "Pakistani mosque Berlin" or "Urdu khutbah Berlin" in Facebook groups for current recommendations, as mosque schedules and language offerings change over time.

**Community Organizations**

- **Pakistanis in Berlin** (@pakistaninberlin on Instagram) — Active community page sharing events, restaurant reviews, and community news
- Pakistani student societies at TU Berlin, FU Berlin, and HU Berlin

**Tips for Berlin**

Berlin is the most affordable major German city for rent and dining out. The Pakistani food scene here is arguably the best in Germany. Check our [cost of living guide](/blog/cost-of-living-germany-2026-pakistanis/) and [housing guide](/blog/finding-housing-germany-guide-pakistanis/) for Berlin-specific tips.

### Frankfurt am Main

Frankfurt and the broader Rhein-Main area is the heartland of the Pakistani community in Germany. Almost a third of all Pakistanis in Germany live in Hesse, and Frankfurt is the centre of gravity.

**Pakistani and South Asian Restaurants**

Frankfurt's Pakistani food scene reflects the size of its community. You will find everything from casual takeaway joints to family-style restaurants. The area around the Hauptbahnhof (main train station) and the Bahnhofsviertel neighbourhood has several South Asian restaurants. Offenbach, just east of Frankfurt, also has a significant Pakistani population with its own restaurant scene.

**Halal Grocery Stores**

Frankfurt is exceptionally well-served for halal groceries. The Münchener Strasse area near the Hauptbahnhof has multiple halal butchers and South Asian grocery stores. Pakistani staples — Shan masala, National spices, Mehran mixes, basmati rice brands like Guard or Falak — are readily available.

**Mosques**

- **Pak Darul Islam Mosque** — One of the most important Pakistani mosques in Germany, established in 1979. This mosque offers Friday sermons (khutbahs) in both German and Urdu, making it a key gathering point for the Urdu-speaking community. It also serves a diverse group of Muslims from various backgrounds.

Frankfurt's Pakistani mosque infrastructure is the most developed in Germany, reflecting the size and longevity of the community here.

**Community Organizations**

Frankfurt has the most established network of Pakistani community organizations in Germany. The community is well-connected through mosque committees, cultural associations, and professional networks. The **Pakistan German Press Club e.V.** (pakpress.de) is recognized across the community and acts as a bridge between Pakistani associations throughout Germany.

**Tips for Frankfurt**

Frankfurt is expensive for housing but offers the strongest Pakistani community infrastructure in Germany. If being close to a large, established Pakistani community matters to you, the Rhein-Main area is the best choice.

### Munich

Munich's Pakistani community is smaller than Frankfurt's or Berlin's but well-organized and growing, driven partly by the tech industry and the university.

**Pakistani and South Asian Restaurants**

Munich has a growing number of South Asian restaurants. While dedicated Pakistani restaurants are fewer than in Berlin or Frankfurt, several Indian-Pakistani restaurants serve authentic Pakistani dishes. Check Google Maps reviews for mentions of "Pakistani food" or "halal" to find the right spots.

**Halal Grocery Stores**

**Grocera** is a Munich-based startup offering same-day halal grocery delivery (order by 16:00). This is a game-changer for those living outside the city centre. Traditional halal shops can be found in the Hauptbahnhof area and in Sendling.

**Mosques**

Munich has several mosques, though finding Urdu-language khutbahs requires some searching. The Islamic community in Munich is predominantly Turkish and Arab, but South Asian mosques do exist. Ask in local Pakistani WhatsApp groups for current recommendations.

**Community Organizations**

- **German Pakistan Cultural Club (GPCC Munich)** (gpcc-munich.de) — Founded in December 2018, GPCC serves as a hub for connecting with Pakistani roots while embracing German culture. They organize cultural events, cricket matches, football tournaments, fitness programs, and mentoring activities for children and teenagers. This is one of the most active Pakistani community organizations in Germany.

**Tips for Munich**

Munich is the most expensive city in Germany, but also offers the highest salaries. The Pakistani community here skews young and professional. GPCC Munich is your best entry point for connecting with the community.

### Hamburg

Hamburg has a well-established Pakistani community with deep roots, particularly around the port and industrial areas.

**Pakistani and South Asian Restaurants**

Hamburg's Pakistani dining scene is centred around the Hauptbahnhof area and St. Georg neighbourhood. Several restaurants serve Pakistani and North Indian cuisine. The community is tight-knit enough that word of mouth is the most reliable way to find the best spots.

**Halal Grocery Stores**

The St. Georg and Steindamm areas have the highest concentration of halal grocery stores and butchers. You can find most Pakistani staples here, including fresh halal meat, spices, and frozen Pakistani snacks.

**Mosques**

- **Pak Islami Majlis Hamburg** (pakislamimajlis.com) — Founded in March 1996, this is Hamburg's primary Pakistani mosque and community centre. Located at Brennerstrasse 12, the mosque has grown with the community and offers Urdu language lessons taught by specialist teachers, in addition to regular prayers and Friday khutbahs. This is the single best place to connect with the Pakistani community in Hamburg.

**Community Organizations**

Pak Islami Majlis serves as both a religious and community centre. Beyond prayers, they organize cultural events, language classes, and community gatherings. With approximately 1,900 Pakistanis in Hamburg, the community is small enough that everyone knows everyone — which is both comforting and, occasionally, a bit much.

**Tips for Hamburg**

Hamburg is a port city with a cosmopolitan feel. The cost of living is moderate compared to Munich but higher than Berlin. The community is warm and welcoming to newcomers.

### Cologne (Köln)

Cologne has a diverse Muslim population and a growing Pakistani community, particularly around the university.

**Pakistani and South Asian Restaurants**

Cologne's Eigelstein neighbourhood near the Hauptbahnhof is a good starting point for South Asian dining. The Ehrenfeld district also has several halal restaurants and grocery stores. Search Google Maps for "Pakistani restaurant Köln" for current options.

**Halal Grocery Stores**

The Keupstrasse area and Eigelstein have multiple halal supermarkets and butchers. Turkish-run halal shops are the most common, but they carry many Pakistani staples (spices, rice, lentils). For specifically Pakistani brands, online stores like Tukwila and Sta Dukaan deliver to Cologne.

**Mosques**

Cologne is home to Germany's largest mosque, the **DITIB Central Mosque** (Zentralmoschee Köln), though sermons there are primarily in Turkish. For Urdu-language services, connect with the local Pakistani community through Facebook groups to find smaller mosques or prayer rooms that serve the South Asian community.

**Community Organizations**

The Pakistani student community at the University of Cologne is active. Look for the Pakistani Students Association on social media.

### Stuttgart

Stuttgart's Pakistani community is concentrated around the automotive industry, with many professionals working at Porsche, Mercedes-Benz, and Bosch.

**Pakistani and South Asian Restaurants**

Stuttgart has several South Asian restaurants, particularly in the city centre and around Bad Cannstatt. The dining scene is smaller than Berlin or Frankfurt, but you can find good Pakistani food if you know where to look. Ask in community WhatsApp groups for current recommendations.

**Halal Grocery Stores**

The area around Stuttgart Hauptbahnhof and Bad Cannstatt has halal grocery stores. Palmyra, a chain of halal supermarkets originally from Aachen, has expanded across Germany and may have locations near Stuttgart.

**Mosques**

Stuttgart has a number of mosques, with the South Asian community served by smaller prayer rooms and community spaces. Connect with locals through community groups for Urdu khutbah locations.

**Community Organizations**

The Pakistani community in Stuttgart is professional-leaning, with many members connected through workplace networks at the major automotive companies. The **Pakistanischer Kulturverein** (Pakistani Cultural Association) in nearby Darmstadt organizes events that draw attendees from across the region, including cricket tournaments, cultural programs, and community gatherings.

### Düsseldorf

Düsseldorf and the broader North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW) region has a significant Pakistani population spread across several cities including Dortmund, Essen, and Duisburg.

**Pakistani and South Asian Restaurants**

Düsseldorf's Oberbilk and Hauptbahnhof areas have the highest concentration of South Asian restaurants and takeaways. NRW in general is well-served — nearby Dortmund has an active **Pakistani Community Germany (PCG)** chapter that regularly shares restaurant recommendations.

**Halal Grocery Stores**

NRW is one of the best-served regions for halal groceries in Germany. **Palmyra**, originally from Aachen, is one of the largest halal meat and grocery chains in the country. **Al Wahid Tandoor Express** (alwahidexpress.com) offers both Pakistani food and groceries. The density of halal shops in NRW means you are never far from Pakistani ingredients.

**Mosques**

NRW has hundreds of mosques. The Pakistani community has established prayer spaces in several cities across the region. The Urdu-speaking community is large enough in NRW that finding a mosque with Urdu khutbahs is easier here than in most other parts of Germany.

**Community Organizations**

- **Pakistani Community Germany (PCG)** — Based in Dortmund, this is one of the most active organizations serving Pakistanis across NRW and beyond. They organize community events, provide support for newcomers, and maintain active social media presence.

## Celebrating Eid and Ramadan in Germany

One of the biggest adjustments for Pakistani Muslims in Germany is adapting to Eid and Ramadan away from home. Here is how to make it feel as close to home as possible.

### Finding Prayer Times

German prayer times vary dramatically by season. In summer, Fajr can be as early as 3:00 AM and Isha as late as 11:30 PM, making Ramadan fasts exceptionally long (up to 18-19 hours). In winter, fasts are much shorter (around 8-9 hours).

- **Muslim Pro** and **Athan** apps provide accurate prayer times for your exact location in Germany
- Most mosques publish their prayer schedules on their websites or in their WhatsApp groups
- The Islamic Council of Germany (Islamrat) and DITIB also publish prayer time tables

### Eid Prayer Locations

For Eid prayers, many cities organize large congregational prayers beyond the regular mosque capacity:

- **Large mosques** hold multiple Eid prayer sessions to accommodate the crowd
- **Convention centres and sports halls** are rented in many cities for Eid prayers — these accommodate thousands and create a more festive atmosphere
- **Open-air prayers** are organized in some cities, weather permitting
- Check your local mosque's announcements starting two weeks before Eid for exact times and locations

The best way to find Eid prayers near you: join your city's Pakistani or Muslim WhatsApp group. Announcements circulate weeks in advance.

### Getting Halal Meat for Qurbani (Eid al-Adha)

Qurbani in Germany requires planning ahead:

- **Halal butchers and farms** offer Qurbani services where you can order a share of a sheep, goat, or cow. These are typically organized through mosques or community WhatsApp groups.
- **Pre-order is essential.** Qurbani slots fill up weeks in advance. Book at least 2-3 weeks before Eid al-Adha.
- **Community-organized Qurbani:** Many mosque communities collectively organize Qurbani at approved slaughterhouses, handling the logistics for you. You pay your share and pick up the meat.
- **Online Qurbani services:** Some organizations facilitate Qurbani back in Pakistan or in other countries if you prefer to donate there.
- **German law:** Animal slaughter in Germany is regulated. Qurbani must be performed at licensed facilities. Backyard slaughter is illegal. Your mosque community will know the approved locations.

### Making Ramadan and Eid Feel Like Home

- **Community iftars:** Most mosques organize community iftars, especially on weekends. These are the closest thing to the iftar atmosphere back home.
- **Cook together:** Organize potluck iftars with your Pakistani friends. Everyone brings one dish — you will have a full dastarkhan (spread) in no time.
- **Eid clothes:** Order from Pakistani online stores 3-4 weeks before Eid to account for shipping time. Khaadi, Gul Ahmed, and Junaid Jamshed ship internationally.
- **Eidi for kids:** Keep the tradition alive. Prepare envelopes and small gifts.
- **Eid meetups:** Pakistani community groups organize Eid Milan (get-togethers) with food, music, and kids' activities.

## Dealing with Homesickness: Practical Tips

Homesickness hits everyone, even if you are excited about being in Germany. It does not mean you made the wrong decision — it means you are human. Here are things that actually help:

### Stay Connected, But Set Boundaries

- **Regular video calls** with family back home — but do not let them become guilt trips. Set a schedule (e.g., every Sunday evening) so both sides know when to expect the call.
- **Send photos and updates** proactively. When your family can picture your life in Germany, they worry less.
- **Time zone management:** Pakistan is 4 hours ahead of Germany (3 hours during daylight saving). Lunch break in Germany is evening chai time in Pakistan — a good window for quick calls.

### Build Your Local Support System

- **Find your people early.** Join a Pakistani community group, attend mosque events, visit a Pakistani restaurant. Having even 2-3 friends who understand your background makes an enormous difference.
- **University Pakistani societies** organize regular meetups, cricket matches, and cultural events. Even if you are not a student, many welcome community members.
- **Do not isolate yourself.** The temptation to stay home after work and call Pakistan is strong. Resist it. Get out and build a life here.

### Practical Comforts

- **Cook Pakistani food.** Nothing cures homesickness like the smell of biryani on the stove. Find your local halal grocery store (see the city guides above) and stock your kitchen.
- **Watch Pakistani content.** YouTube, ARY, Geo, HUM — all available online. Having familiar sounds in your apartment helps.
- **Celebrate every occasion.** Pakistan Day (23 March), Independence Day (14 August), Eid, Shab-e-Barat — mark them all. Gather with friends, cook special food, dress up. These celebrations matter more abroad than they do at home.

### Know When to Get Help

Persistent homesickness that affects your work, sleep, or appetite for more than a few weeks may be depression. Germany has a strong mental health support system. Your health insurance covers therapy sessions — and yes, you can find therapists who speak Urdu or at least English. Ask your Hausarzt (family doctor) for a referral or search on **Doctolib** for therapists with the relevant language skills.

## Raising Kids as Pakistani-German

If you have children or are planning to, raising them with a dual Pakistani-German identity is one of the most rewarding — and sometimes challenging — parts of expat life.

### Language

- **Speak Urdu at home.** Children pick up German naturally from school, kindergarten, and friends. Your job is to give them Urdu. Speak it consistently at home.
- **Weekend Urdu classes:** Some community organizations and mosques offer weekend Urdu language schools. GPCC Munich, for example, runs children's programs. Ask at your local mosque.
- **Books and media in Urdu:** Order Urdu storybooks online. Let them watch Pakistani cartoons and kids' shows alongside German ones.

### Islamic Education

- **Mosque Quran classes:** Most mosques in Germany offer weekend or after-school Quran classes for children. Quality varies — visit the mosque, talk to the teacher, and check that the environment is positive and age-appropriate.
- **Islamic schools:** A few German cities have full-time Islamic schools, though these are predominantly in Arabic or Turkish. Supplementary weekend Islamic education through mosques is more common for the Pakistani community.
- **Home education:** Many Pakistani families supplement mosque classes with home-based Quran and Islamic studies teaching.

### Identity and Belonging

- **Both cultures, not one or the other.** Your children will be Pakistani-German, and that is a strength, not a conflict. Expose them to both cultures proudly.
- **Community matters.** Bring your kids to Pakistani community events, Eid celebrations, and mosque gatherings. Let them grow up knowing other Pakistani-German kids.
- **School integration:** German schools are generally welcoming of cultural diversity. Talk to teachers about accommodating Eid holidays, halal food at school events, and Ramadan adjustments.
- **Cricket, not just Fussball.** Sign them up for a local cricket club if there is one — many Pakistani community organizations run youth cricket programs. But let them love Fussball too.

## Finding Pakistani Doctors Who Speak Urdu

When you are sick, the last thing you want is to struggle with medical vocabulary in German. Here is how to find doctors who speak Urdu:

- **Doctolib** (doctolib.de) — Germany's largest doctor-booking platform. You can filter by language. Search for "Urdu" in the language filter to find doctors who speak it.
- **Jameda** (jameda.de) — Another doctor review platform with language filters.
- **Google Maps** — Search "Pakistani doctor [your city]" or "Urdu speaking doctor [your city]". Read reviews in which patients mention language.
- **Community recommendations** — Ask in your Pakistani WhatsApp or Facebook group. Community members will have tried-and-tested recommendations.
- **Your Hausarzt** — Your general practitioner is your entry point to the German healthcare system. Finding one who speaks Urdu (or at least English) makes life significantly easier. Once you have a Hausarzt, they refer you to specialists as needed.

Many Pakistani-origin doctors practice in Germany, particularly in cities with large Pakistani communities like Frankfurt and the Rhein-Main area. Dentists, GPs, and specialists of Pakistani origin can often be found through community networks.
