Pakistan is one of the largest remittance-receiving countries in the world. In the fiscal year 2024-25, overseas Pakistanis sent over $30 billion home through official channels - and a significant share of that comes from Europe, including Germany. If you are a Pakistani working in Germany, sending money home is not optional. It is part of life. Whether you are supporting family, paying off a loan, building a house, or investing back home, you need a reliable, cheap, and fast way to move euros into Pakistani rupees.

The problem? Most people stick with whatever method they tried first - often a bank wire or Western Union - and never compare. That can cost you hundreds of euros per year in unnecessary fees and bad exchange rates. This guide compares every major option available in Germany in 2026, with real numbers, so you can make an informed choice.

## Why the Transfer Method Matters More Than You Think

Let us say you send €500 home every month. The difference between the cheapest and most expensive method can be **€15-25 per transfer**. Over a year, that is **€180-300** lost to fees and poor exchange rates. For someone sending €1,000 monthly, the annual difference can exceed **€500**.

The cost of a money transfer has two components:

1. **The transfer fee** - the upfront charge the service takes
2. **The exchange rate markup** - the hidden cost where the provider gives you a worse rate than the real mid-market rate and keeps the difference

Many services advertise "zero fees" but bury the cost in a terrible exchange rate. Always check the total amount your recipient will receive in PKR - that is the only number that matters.

## The Mid-Market Rate: Your Benchmark

Before comparing services, you need to know the **mid-market exchange rate** (also called the interbank rate). This is the real exchange rate that banks use when trading currencies with each other. No service will give you this exact rate - they all add a markup - but the closer they get, the better the deal.

As of March 2026, the mid-market EUR to PKR rate is approximately **1 EUR = 325 PKR**. You can check the live rate on Google, XE.com, or Wise at any time.

When a service offers you 318 PKR per euro instead of 325, that 2.2% difference on a €1,000 transfer means your family receives **7,000 PKR less**. That is real money.

## Complete Comparison: All Major Transfer Options

Here is every significant way to send money from Germany to Pakistan in 2026, ranked from cheapest to most expensive.

### 1. Wise (formerly TransferWise) - Best Overall

Wise is the gold standard for international transfers and consistently offers the best deal for EUR to PKR.

**How it works:** Wise uses the real mid-market exchange rate and charges a small, transparent fee on top. No hidden markup, no surprises.

- **Fee:** Approximately 0.6-0.7% of the transfer amount when paying by bank transfer (SEPA). Card payments cost slightly more (around 1.2-1.5%)
- **Exchange rate:** Mid-market rate (the best you can get)
- **Speed:** 1-2 business days (often same-day for bank deposits in Pakistan)
- **Delivery options:** Bank deposit to any Pakistani bank account
- **Payment methods:** Bank transfer (SEPA), debit card, credit card
- **Minimum transfer:** €1
- **Maximum transfer:** Up to €1,000,000 (verification required for large amounts)

**Why Wise wins:** The combination of mid-market rate plus a low percentage fee almost always results in the most PKR received. Even when a competitor advertises "zero fees," Wise still delivers more rupees because those competitors hide costs in the exchange rate.

### 2. Remitly - Best for Speed and Mobile Wallets

Remitly is a strong competitor, especially popular for sending to Pakistan. They offer delivery to bank accounts, JazzCash, Easypaisa, and cash pickup.

- **Fee:** €0-3.99 depending on transfer amount, payment method, and speed tier
- **Exchange rate:** 1-2.5% markup over mid-market rate (varies by delivery speed)
- **Speed:** Minutes to 3 business days depending on the tier chosen
  - *Express:* Minutes (higher exchange rate markup)
  - *Economy:* 3-5 business days (better rate)
- **Delivery options:** Bank deposit, JazzCash, Easypaisa, cash pickup
- **Payment methods:** Bank transfer, debit card, credit card
- **First transfer:** Often fee-free with a promotional exchange rate on your first €1,000

**Best for:** People who need mobile wallet delivery (JazzCash/Easypaisa) or cash pickup at locations across Pakistan. Also a good option if your recipient does not have a bank account.

### 3. Xe (formerly Azimo) - Good Alternative

Xe acquired Azimo in 2023 and now operates remittance services under the Xe brand in Europe.

- **Fee:** Often €0 for bank transfers (cost built into exchange rate)
- **Exchange rate:** 1-2% markup over mid-market rate
- **Speed:** 1-3 business days
- **Delivery options:** Bank deposit, cash pickup
- **Payment methods:** Bank transfer, debit card

**Best for:** A decent middle-ground option. Check the total PKR received against Wise before committing.

### 4. Western Union - Best for Cash Pickup Network

Western Union has the largest agent network in Pakistan with over 20,000 locations. If your recipient needs cash in hand, especially in smaller cities or rural areas, Western Union is hard to beat for accessibility.

- **Fee:** €1.99-10.99 depending on amount and payment method
- **Exchange rate:** 2-5% markup over mid-market rate
- **Speed:** Minutes for cash pickup; 1-3 days for bank deposit
- **Delivery options:** Cash pickup (massive network), bank deposit
- **Payment methods:** Bank transfer, debit/credit card, cash at agent locations in Germany

**Best for:** Cash pickup in remote areas of Pakistan. Not recommended for regular transfers due to high overall costs.

### 5. MoneyGram - Cash Pickup Alternative

Similar to Western Union but with a slightly smaller network in Pakistan.

- **Fee:** €1.99-9.99 depending on amount and method
- **Exchange rate:** 2-4% markup over mid-market rate
- **Speed:** Minutes for cash pickup; 1-2 days for bank transfer
- **Delivery options:** Cash pickup, bank deposit, JazzCash, Easypaisa
- **Payment methods:** Bank transfer, card, cash at agent locations

**Best for:** An alternative to Western Union for cash pickup, sometimes with slightly better rates.

### 6. Bank SWIFT Transfer (Sparkasse, Deutsche Bank, Commerzbank, etc.)

Sending money through your German bank via SWIFT is the most expensive option and should be avoided for regular remittances.

- **Fee:** €15-45 per transfer (sending fee) + possible intermediary bank fees (€10-25) + receiving bank fee in Pakistan (PKR 500-1,500)
- **Exchange rate:** 3-6% markup over mid-market rate
- **Speed:** 3-5 business days
- **Delivery options:** Bank deposit only
- **Payment methods:** From your German bank account

**Best for:** Almost never the best option. Only consider for very large transfers (€10,000+) where the flat fee becomes a smaller percentage, or when the receiving party specifically requires a SWIFT transfer for documentation purposes.

## Receiving Options in Pakistan

### Bank Deposit

The most common and recommended method. Your recipient needs:
- A Pakistani bank account (savings or current)
- The bank's IBAN or account number
- Bank name and branch code (some services only need the IBAN)

**Recommended Pakistani banks for receiving:**

- **HBL (Habib Bank Limited)** - Pakistan's largest bank, extensive branch and ATM network, reliable for receiving international transfers
- **UBL (United Bank Limited)** - Strong digital banking, UBL Digital app works well for tracking incoming transfers
- **MCB (Muslim Commercial Bank)** - Wide branch network, good for recipients in smaller cities
- **Meezan Bank** - Pakistan's largest Islamic bank, a popular choice for those who prefer Shariah-compliant banking
- **Allied Bank / Bank Alfalah** - Both have extensive networks and handle remittances efficiently

**Tip:** Make sure your recipient's bank account is in PKR (rupee account), not a foreign currency account, unless you specifically want them to receive in euros or dollars.

### JazzCash

JazzCash is Pakistan's largest mobile wallet with over 121,000 agent locations. Receiving international remittances on JazzCash is straightforward:

- Recipient needs a registered and active JazzCash account (linked to their CNIC)
- Money arrives directly into the mobile wallet
- Can be cashed out at any JazzCash agent or used for bill payments, mobile top-ups, and online shopping
- **Supported by:** Remitly, MoneyGram, WorldRemit, and others

**Best for:** Recipients who do not have bank accounts or who live in areas with limited bank branches.

### Easypaisa

Similar to JazzCash, Easypaisa is another major mobile wallet in Pakistan:

- Recipient needs a registered Easypaisa account
- Instant receipt of funds
- Cash-out at Easypaisa agent locations
- **Supported by:** Remitly, MoneyGram, WorldRemit

### Cash Pickup

Your recipient walks into a Western Union or MoneyGram agent location with their CNIC and a reference number, and collects cash. No bank account needed.

- **Western Union:** Over 20,000 locations in Pakistan
- **MoneyGram:** Over 10,000 locations
- **Best for:** Urgent transfers, recipients without bank accounts, small towns

## Step-by-Step: Setting Up Wise (Our Recommended Service)

Here is how to set up Wise in Germany and send your first transfer to Pakistan.

### Step 1: Create Your Wise Account

1. Go to [wise.com](https://wise.com) or download the Wise app (iOS / Android)
2. Sign up with your email address or Google/Apple account
3. Select Germany as your country of residence

### Step 2: Verify Your Identity

1. Upload a photo of your passport or German Aufenthaltstitel (residence permit)
2. Take a selfie for identity verification
3. Provide your German address (Anmeldung address)
4. Verification usually takes a few hours but can take up to 2 business days

### Step 3: Set Up Your First Transfer

1. Click "Send money"
2. Enter the amount in EUR (e.g., €500)
3. Select PKR as the recipient currency
4. Wise immediately shows you:
   - The exact fee (e.g., €3.25)
   - The exchange rate (mid-market rate)
   - The exact amount your recipient will get in PKR
5. Review and confirm

### Step 4: Add Your Recipient

1. Enter your recipient's full name (must match their bank account name exactly)
2. Enter their Pakistani bank account number or IBAN
3. Select their bank from the dropdown (HBL, UBL, MCB, Meezan, etc.)

### Step 5: Pay for the Transfer

1. Choose your payment method:
   - **Bank transfer (SEPA):** Cheapest option, takes a few hours to process
   - **Debit card:** Slightly higher fee, instant processing
   - **Credit card:** Highest fee, instant processing
2. For SEPA, Wise gives you their bank details - transfer from your German bank (Sparkasse, N26, ING, etc.)
3. Once Wise receives your payment, conversion happens at the mid-market rate

### Step 6: Track and Confirm

1. Track the transfer status in the Wise app or website
2. You get notifications at each step: payment received, converted, sent, delivered
3. Your recipient gets the PKR in their bank account, usually within 1-2 business days

**Pro tip:** If you send money regularly, save your recipient's details. Future transfers take less than 2 minutes.

## Tips for Getting the Best Exchange Rate

### 1. Always Compare Before Sending

Never assume the same service is cheapest every time. Exchange rate markups fluctuate. Spend 2 minutes checking Wise and Remitly before each transfer. Use the "recipient gets" amount as your comparison metric - not the fee or rate in isolation.

### 2. Send by Bank Transfer, Not Card

Paying by SEPA bank transfer is almost always cheaper than debit or credit card. The fee difference can be 0.5-1% of the transfer amount. If you are not in a rush, always choose bank transfer.

### 3. Send Larger Amounts Less Frequently

If you send €500 every month, consider sending €1,000 every two months instead. The flat fee component becomes proportionally smaller on larger transfers. Of course, only do this if your family's cash flow allows it.

### 4. Avoid Weekends and Holidays

Currency markets are closed on weekends. Some services process weekend transfers at Monday's rate, which may be different from what you saw on Friday. For the most predictable rates, initiate transfers during European banking hours on weekdays.

### 5. Set Rate Alerts

Wise and Xe both let you set exchange rate alerts. If you have flexibility on timing, set an alert for your target rate (e.g., "notify me when EUR/PKR crosses 330") and send when the rate is favorable.

### 6. Never Use Airport or Hotel Currency Exchange

This should go without saying, but airport exchange counters offer rates that are 5-10% worse than online services. Never use them for remittances.

## Tax Implications: What You Need to Know

### Germany Side

**Germany does not tax remittances.** Sending money to your family in Pakistan is not a taxable event in Germany. There is no limit on how much you can send, and you do not need to report personal remittances on your German tax return.

However, if you are sending very large amounts (€12,500 or more in a single transaction or within a month), German banks are required to report this to the Bundesbank for statistical purposes under the Außenwirtschaftsverordnung (Foreign Trade Regulation). This is an automatic reporting requirement - it does not trigger any tax liability, but be aware that large transfers are tracked.

### Pakistan Side

**Pakistan does not tax incoming remittances through official channels** - but documentation matters.

- Foreign remittances up to **PKR 5 million per year** (approximately €15,000) received through official banking channels are exempt from income tax without detailed source documentation
- For amounts exceeding PKR 5 million, the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) may ask for source documentation (employment contract, salary slips, bank statements from Germany)
- Remittances through **unofficial channels (hawala/hundi)** are illegal and may attract a 10% withholding tax if discovered, plus legal consequences
- Your recipient should keep **bank encashment certificates** for all incoming remittances - these serve as proof that the money came through legal channels and may be needed for tax filings or property purchases

**Critical advice:** Always send money through official, regulated channels (Wise, Remitly, Western Union, bank transfer). Apart from the legal issues, official remittances help Pakistan's foreign exchange reserves and contribute to the country's economic data. The State Bank of Pakistan actively encourages formal remittance channels and has made it easier than ever through initiatives like the Roshan Digital Account.

## Roshan Digital Account: A Special Option for Overseas Pakistanis

The **Roshan Digital Account (RDA)** is a State Bank of Pakistan initiative launched specifically for Non-Resident Pakistanis (NRPs). It is not a remittance service per se, but it provides a Pakistani bank account that you can open and manage entirely from Germany - no need to visit Pakistan.

### What RDA Offers

- **Open a Pakistani bank account remotely** with banks like HBL, UBL, Meezan, MCB, Allied Bank, Standard Chartered, and others
- **Send money to your own account** in Pakistan via SWIFT transfer (reduced correspondent bank fees - typically $5-9 per transaction)
- **Invest in Naya Pakistan Certificates** - fixed-income government securities with attractive returns (denominated in PKR or USD)
- **Invest in the Pakistan Stock Exchange** directly
- **Buy property in Pakistan** with full repatriation rights (you can bring the sale proceeds back to Germany)
- **Full repatriation** - you can move money back out of Pakistan without SBP approval, up to the balance in your RDA

### Who Should Use RDA?

RDA is not ideal for regular family remittances (SWIFT fees are higher than Wise, and the process is slower). But it is excellent if you want to:

- Invest in Pakistani government bonds (Naya Pakistan Certificates offer competitive rates)
- Buy property in Pakistan with legal protection and repatriation rights
- Maintain a Pakistani bank account for your own use when visiting
- Invest in the Pakistan Stock Exchange from Germany

## Regular vs. One-Time Transfers: Which Approach?

### Regular Monthly Transfers

If you send money home every month (as most Pakistani expats do), here is the optimal setup:

1. **Primary service: Wise** - Set up your recipient once, send via SEPA bank transfer each month. Lowest total cost for bank-to-bank transfers
2. **Backup service: Remitly** - Keep an account active for situations where you need faster delivery or mobile wallet delivery (JazzCash/Easypaisa)
3. **Emergency backup: Western Union** - For urgent cash pickup when your family needs money within minutes

**Monthly routine:** On payday (or whenever you send), open Wise, enter the amount, confirm, and pay by bank transfer. The whole process takes under 3 minutes once set up.

### One-Time Large Transfers

For large transfers (buying property, car, wedding expenses, etc.):

1. **Compare rates carefully** - the exchange rate difference on €5,000-20,000 is significant
2. **Consider splitting across 2-3 days** - if the amount is very large and you want to average out exchange rate fluctuations
3. **Use Wise for amounts up to €10,000** - still the best rate in most cases
4. **Consider RDA for investment transfers** - especially if you want repatriation rights
5. **Document everything** - keep screenshots of rates, confirmation emails, and bank statements for both German and Pakistani tax records

## Common Mistakes Pakistani Expats Make

### 1. Using Their German Bank for SWIFT Transfers

Your Sparkasse or Deutsche Bank will happily send a SWIFT transfer to Pakistan - and charge you €25-40 in fees plus a terrible exchange rate. Use Wise instead and save 70-80% on costs.

### 2. Not Checking the Exchange Rate

"Zero fee" means nothing if the exchange rate is 3% worse than mid-market. Always compare the total PKR received, not just the advertised fee.

### 3. Using Hawala/Hundi

Informal money transfer channels are illegal in both Germany and Pakistan. Beyond the legal risk, you have zero protection if the money disappears. And you are depriving Pakistan of official foreign exchange reserves that the country desperately needs.

### 4. Sending Cash with Travelers

Carrying more than €10,000 in cash across borders without declaring it is illegal under EU regulations. Undeclared cash can be seized. Even amounts under €10,000 can raise questions. Use official digital channels instead.

### 5. Never Comparing Services

The cheapest service last year might not be cheapest this year. Remittance companies frequently adjust their fees and exchange rate markups. Spend 2 minutes comparing before each transfer.
