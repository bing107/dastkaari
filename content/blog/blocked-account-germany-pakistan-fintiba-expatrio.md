If you are a Pakistani student or job seeker planning to move to Germany, one of the first things you need to sort out is a **blocked account** (*Sperrkonto*). It is a mandatory requirement for your German visa application, and you cannot skip it.

This guide covers everything you need to know: what a blocked account is, the 2026 required amount, a detailed comparison of the top providers, and practical advice for transferring money from Pakistan to Germany - including how to deal with SBP regulations and SWIFT transfers.

## What Is a Blocked Account (Sperrkonto)?

A **blocked account** (*Sperrkonto* in German) is a special type of bank account required by the German government as **proof of financial resources**. It shows the German embassy or consulate that you have enough money to support yourself during your stay in Germany.

Unlike a regular bank account, the money in a blocked account is "locked." You cannot withdraw the full amount at once. Instead, a fixed monthly amount is released to your regular German bank account after you arrive in Germany and activate the disbursement.

### Who Needs a Blocked Account?

You need a blocked account if you are applying for:

- A **German student visa** (the most common use case for Pakistanis)
- A **job seeker visa** (see our [Job Seeker Visa guide](/blog/germany-job-seeker-visa-pakistan/))
- A **language course visa** for stays longer than 90 days
- Any other national visa where proof of funds is required

If you are applying for an [EU Blue Card](/blog/germany-blue-card-guide-pakistanis/), you typically prove finances through your employment contract and salary instead.

## 2026 Required Amount: EUR 11,904

The German government sets the blocked account amount based on the **BAfoG rate** - the national standard for the minimum amount a student needs to live in Germany.

As of **2026**, the required amount is:

| Period | Amount |
|--------|--------|
| Per month | **EUR 992** |
| Per year (12 months) | **EUR 11,904** |

This amount has remained stable since the 2024 BAfoG update. You must deposit the full annual amount (EUR 11,904) before your visa appointment, plus any setup fees and buffer amounts required by your provider.

**Important:** The EUR 11,904 is the *minimum*. You cannot deposit less. Some embassies may accept a proportional amount if your stay is shorter than 12 months (for example, EUR 5,952 for a 6-month language course), but always confirm with the German embassy in Islamabad or the consulate in Karachi before your appointment.

### What About the Buffer Amount?

Most providers require you to deposit slightly more than EUR 11,904 to cover:

- **Setup fees** (EUR 89–159 depending on the provider)
- **Monthly maintenance fees** (EUR 4.90–9.90/month)
- **Currency buffer** (~EUR 100) to absorb exchange rate fluctuations and intermediary bank charges during your SWIFT transfer

Plan to transfer approximately **EUR 12,200–12,300** in total to be safe.

### Our Recommendation for Pakistani Students

**Expatrio** is the best overall choice for most Pakistani applicants for three reasons:

1. **Lowest total cost.** At EUR 89 setup + EUR 5/month, you save over EUR 100 compared to Fintiba in the first year alone.
2. **Free bank account included.** The Expatrio Bank Account is automatically connected to your blocked account, so your monthly disbursements go straight into a usable account. With Fintiba, you need to open a separate German bank account.

**Coracle** is the cheapest option if you only need the blocked account without monthly fees. However, it is newer and less widely discussed in Pakistani student communities, so you may find fewer Urdu/English guides and peer experiences.

## Step-by-Step: How to Open a Blocked Account from Pakistan

The entire process is done online. You do **not** need to visit any German office or bank in person. Here is how it works, using Expatrio as the example (Fintiba and Coracle follow a similar process):

### Step 1: Register Online (10 minutes)

1. Go to [Expatrio.com](https://www.expatrio.com) (or [Fintiba.com](https://www.fintiba.com) if you prefer Fintiba)
2. Click "Open Blocked Account"
3. Fill in your personal details - name exactly as it appears on your passport
4. Upload a scan or clear photo of your **passport** (this is the only document you need for registration)
5. Select your start date and duration (usually 12 months)
6. Choose whether you want the Value Package (health insurance bundle)

### Step 2: Verify Your Identity

- Expatrio and Fintiba use **video identification** - you will do a short video call where you show your passport
- Alternatively, some providers accept **document upload verification**
- This step usually takes 15–30 minutes and can be done from Pakistan

### Step 3: Receive Your Account Details

Within **24 hours** of verification, you receive:

- Your blocked account IBAN (German bank account number)
- SWIFT/BIC code
- The exact amount to transfer (EUR 11,904 + fees + buffer)
- Instructions for the bank transfer

### Step 4: Transfer Money from Pakistan (The Critical Step)

This is where it gets Pakistan-specific. See the detailed section below on [transferring money from Pakistan](#transferring-money-from-pakistan-to-your-blocked-account).

### Step 5: Receive Your Blocking Confirmation

Once the full amount arrives in your blocked account (usually 3–7 business days after the SWIFT transfer), the provider issues a **Blocking Confirmation** (*Sperrbescheinigung*). This is the official document you need for your visa appointment.

- Expatrio: Issued within hours of receiving your funds
- Fintiba: Issued within 1–2 business days of receiving your funds

**Print this document** and bring it to your visa appointment at the German embassy in Islamabad or consulate in Karachi.

### Step 6: Attend Your Visa Appointment

Present the Blocking Confirmation along with your other visa documents. The embassy will verify it as part of your financial proof.

## Transferring Money from Pakistan to Your Blocked Account

This is the part that causes the most stress for Pakistani students. Sending EUR 12,000+ from Pakistan involves navigating SBP (State Bank of Pakistan) regulations, high exchange rate spreads, and slow SWIFT transfers. Here is what you need to know.

### SBP Regulations on Education Remittances

The State Bank of Pakistan allows **authorized dealer banks** (your commercial bank) to remit foreign exchange for education abroad. The key rules:

- **Annual limit:** Up to **USD 70,000** (or equivalent in EUR) per student per calendar year for tuition fees, living expenses, and related costs. Your blocked account amount of ~EUR 12,200 is well within this limit.
- **Cash allowance:** Authorized dealers can also release up to **USD 5,000** in cash foreign exchange, endorsed on your passport, for initial expenses like boarding and lodging upon arrival.
- **Required documentation:** Your bank will ask for specific documents (detailed below).

### Documents Your Pakistani Bank Will Need

When you go to your bank to initiate the SWIFT transfer, bring:

1. **Your CNIC** (Computerized National Identity Card)
2. **Parent/guardian's CNIC** (if the account is in their name or they are funding the transfer)
3. **Your passport** (valid, with at least 6 months remaining)
4. **University admission letter** (from your German university)
5. **Cost sheet or fee breakdown** from the university showing expenses
6. **Blocked account details** - the IBAN, SWIFT/BIC code, and exact amount from your provider (Expatrio/Fintiba)
7. **Form M** (foreign exchange declaration form, your bank will provide this)

**Pro tip:** Some banks may not be familiar with blocked accounts. Print out the Expatrio or Fintiba account details page and explain that this is a mandatory German government requirement for student visas. Bring a printout of the German embassy's website page on blocked accounts as backup.

### SWIFT Transfer Tips for Pakistanis

1. **Initiate the transfer early.** SWIFT transfers from Pakistan to Germany take **5–10 business days**. During peak visa season (May–August), it can take longer. Start at least 2–3 weeks before your visa appointment.

2. **Send in EUR, not USD.** Tell your bank to send the transfer in **euros**. If you send USD, the German receiving bank may convert it to EUR at an unfavorable rate, and you could end up short of the required amount.

3. **Add a buffer of EUR 50–100 extra.** Intermediary banks (correspondent banks between your Pakistani bank and the German bank) may deduct charges. If you send exactly EUR 11,904 and a correspondent bank deducts EUR 20, you will be short and your blocking confirmation will be delayed.

4. **Use the OUR (charges on sender) option.** When filling out the SWIFT form at your bank, select **"OUR"** for the charges option. This means all transfer fees are paid by you (the sender) upfront, so the full amount arrives at the destination. The alternatives - "BEN" (beneficiary pays) or "SHA" (shared) - may result in deductions from the transferred amount.

5. **Get the SWIFT reference number.** After initiating the transfer, get the **MT103 SWIFT confirmation** or at minimum the transaction reference number from your bank. You can provide this to Expatrio/Fintiba so they can trace your incoming payment if there are delays.

6. **Check the exchange rate.** Pakistani banks apply a spread (markup) on the EUR/PKR rate. The State Bank reference rate is usually 1–3% lower than what you will actually pay. Compare rates between your bank's branch and their online banking portal - sometimes online rates are slightly better.

7. **Timing matters.** Exchange rates fluctuate. The EUR/PKR rate can move significantly in a week. If you have flexibility, watch the rate for a few days before transferring.

## What Happens After You Arrive in Germany

Once you land in Germany and complete your [first 30 days checklist](/blog/first-30-days-germany-checklist-pakistanis/) (including city registration or *Anmeldung*), here is how the blocked account works:

### Activating Monthly Disbursements

1. **Open a regular German bank account** (Girokonto) - if you used Expatrio, you already have the Expatrio Bank Account. Otherwise, open an account with N26, Commerzbank, or your local Sparkasse.
2. **Log in to your Expatrio or Fintiba account** and set up the monthly disbursement to your regular account.
3. **EUR 992/month** will be automatically transferred to your regular account on a set date each month.

### Can You Access More Than EUR 992/Month?

No. The EUR 992 monthly limit is set by German regulations. You cannot withdraw more than this from your blocked account, even in an emergency. This is why having additional savings, a part-time job, or a scholarship is important.

However, once your blocked account is empty (after 12 months), you are no longer bound by this restriction - any new money you earn or receive goes into your regular account.

### Renewing Your Blocked Account

If your study program extends beyond your initial blocked account period, you will need to either:

- **Top up** your existing blocked account with another EUR 11,904 for the next year
- **Open a new** blocked account (some providers charge extension fees - Fintiba charges EUR 159 for extensions)

Your residence permit renewal (*Aufenthaltserlaubnis*) at the Auslanderbehorde will require proof of continued financial resources.

## Closing Your Blocked Account

You can close your blocked account when:

- You have **permanent employment** in Germany and no longer need it for visa purposes
- You are **leaving Germany** and want to transfer the remaining balance back
- Your **residence status changes** (e.g., you get a Blue Card or permanent residency)

To close the account:

1. Log in to your provider's platform
2. Request account closure
3. Provide your bank details for the remaining balance transfer
4. The remaining funds are transferred to your nominated account within a few business days

**Note:** If you are transferring the balance back to Pakistan, SBP regulations on inward remittances apply. The amount will be received in PKR at the prevailing exchange rate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I open a blocked account without a university admission letter?

Yes. The blocked account itself only requires your **passport**. However, your Pakistani bank will need the admission letter to process the SWIFT transfer under education remittance rules. If you are applying for a language course visa, use your language school enrollment letter instead.

### How long does the whole process take from Pakistan?

| Step | Time |
|------|------|
| Account registration + verification | 1–2 days |
| SWIFT transfer from Pakistan | 5–10 business days |
| Blocking confirmation issued | 1–2 days after funds arrive |
| **Total** | **~10–14 business days** |

Start the process **at least 3–4 weeks** before your visa appointment to be safe.

### What if my SWIFT transfer is delayed or lost?

Contact your Pakistani bank first and ask them to **trace the transfer** using the MT103 reference number. Also contact your blocked account provider (Expatrio/Fintiba) with the reference number so they can check with their receiving bank. In most cases, delays are caused by intermediary banks holding the transfer for compliance checks.

### Can my parents transfer the money from their account?

Yes. The SWIFT transfer does not need to come from your own account. A parent or guardian can send it from their bank account. The Pakistani bank will need the parent's CNIC and your relationship documentation.

### Is Wise (TransferWise) a good option for funding the blocked account?

Wise offers competitive exchange rates and lower fees than traditional SWIFT transfers. However, there are two considerations:

1. Wise transfers from Pakistan have limited EUR sending options - check their current availability
2. Some blocked account providers require the transfer to come from an account **in your name**. Verify with your provider before using Wise.

If Wise is available for PKR-to-EUR transfers at the time you need it, it can save you money on exchange rates compared to a traditional bank SWIFT transfer.

### What if I get rejected for the visa? Can I get my money back?

Yes. If your visa is denied, you can close your blocked account and get the full deposited amount back (minus any non-refundable setup fees and monthly charges that have already been deducted). The refund process takes about 1–2 weeks.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The 2026 blocked account requirement is EUR 11,904** (EUR 992/month)
2. **Expatrio is the best value** for Pakistani students - EUR 89 setup, EUR 5/month, free bank account included
3. **Start early** - the SWIFT transfer from Pakistan alone takes 5–10 business days
4. **Send in EUR, use OUR charges, add a buffer** to avoid your transfer arriving short
5. **HBL, UBL, MCB, and Standard Chartered** are the most reliable Pakistani banks for this transfer
6. **Keep your MT103 SWIFT reference** - you will need it if there are delays
7. **Budget approximately PKR 950,000–955,000** total (at current approximate rates - always check the actual rate)

Opening a blocked account feels overwhelming when you are doing it for the first time, but it is a straightforward process once you understand the steps. Thousands of Pakistani students do this every year. Start early, follow the steps above, and you will have your Blocking Confirmation ready for your visa appointment.
